<CaseStudyGrid>

<CaseStudySection title="Context">

Simmer was created to solve a common kitchen problem: home cooks constantly need to check their phones mid-recipe, breaking flow and dirtying their screens, especially when following recipes saved from TikTok, Instagram, or YouTube that aren't formatted for hands-free use.

</CaseStudySection>

<CaseStudySection title="Challenge">

The team had to research and select libraries compatible with their tech stack for real-time audio streaming, then iterate extensively on streaming buffer sizes and speech-to-text activation thresholds to minimize latency and make the voice assistant feel truly conversational.

</CaseStudySection>

<CaseStudySection title="Approach">

The team combined a Flask backend for data handling with a Gemini LLM (orchestrated via LangChain) to transform parsed recipe content into dialogue-style, conversational instructions, then layered in bidirectional audio streaming to keep speech-to-text and text-to-speech responsive in real time.

</CaseStudySection>

<CaseStudySection title="Impact" colSpan={12}>

By combining multi-platform recipe import with true hands-free, conversational voice guidance, Simmer differentiated itself from competitors like Umami and SideChef, which lack real-time Q&A, positioning it to turn saved social media recipes into an actually cookable, guided experience.

</CaseStudySection>
