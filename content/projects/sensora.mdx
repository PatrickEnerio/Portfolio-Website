<CaseStudyGrid>

<CaseStudySection title="Context">

During our hackathon we noticed students struggle to translate time spent into real learning. Sensora was born to surface what actually helps you focus — visualizing your habits, suggesting better sessions, and keeping data private and lightweight.

</CaseStudySection>

<CaseStudySection title="Challenge">

Focus tools often equate hours at a desk with progress, while cloud-based trackers raise privacy concerns and add setup friction. We needed objective, real-time engagement signals that stay on-device, turn into recommendations students can act on immediately, and still ship as a lightweight prototype within a 48-hour hackathon.

</CaseStudySection>

<CaseStudySection title="Approach">

We built a local vision pipeline — camera input, MediaPipe face and eye tracking, and CLIP-based engagement analysis — feeding a Flask backend that stores focus samples and generates study recommendations. A React dashboard visualizes live and historical sessions with a dark, minimal UI designed around the product promise: study sessions shaped by you, for you. No mandatory accounts; analysis stays on the machine.

<Callout type="info">
Recognized with Best Beginner Project at HackAI among all first-time hackathon teams.
</Callout>

</CaseStudySection>

<CaseStudySection title="Impact" colSpan={12}>

Sensora showed judges and teammates that focus feedback can be private, actionable, and light enough to demo under pressure — winning Best Beginner Project at HackAI and giving students a clearer path from time spent to habits that actually support learning.

</CaseStudySection>
