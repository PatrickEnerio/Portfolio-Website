<CaseStudySection title="Context">

Partnered with [TheLab.ms](https://thelab.ms/), a nonprofit makerspace in Richardson, TX, through UTDesign EPICS (Spring 2025). Makerspace members needed a compact, affordable way to form detailed molds without industrial vacuum-forming equipment. Our team — PANBA — set out to deliver a tabletop, open-source vacuum former that hobbyists could build and use safely.

</CaseStudySection>

<CaseStudySection title="Challenge">

Commercial vacuum formers are expensive and oversized for a community workshop. We had one semester to define requirements with an industry partner, design for a standard table footprint, heat and form 5.5" × 8" × 2mm acrylic, and prove the concept with accessible materials and hand tools — while coordinating a five-person, multi-major team.

</CaseStudySection>

<CaseStudySection title="Approach">

As Engineering Project Lead, I owned outcomes and mentored teammates on CAD and testing while we iterated from first to final CAD designs. We characterized temperature and vacuum needs for thermoplastics, built a wood-and-metal vacuum chamber compatible with a standard shop vacuum, wired a custom heat box from a cake pan and toaster-oven elements, and engineered a railing system to move material between heating and forming. Simple controls and clear safety guidance kept the prototype usable for makerspace members.

<Callout type="info">
Awarded Excellence in Design by UTDesign EPICS for dedication to design excellence, project progress, and team collaboration (Spring 2025).
</Callout>

</CaseStudySection>

<CaseStudySection title="Impact" colSpan={12}>

The prototype successfully formed clear plastic molds over everyday objects, giving TheLab.ms a path to low-cost, open-source tooling that does not require industrial equipment. Future work includes tighter machining, broader thermoplastic support, PID temperature control, better insulation, and published build instructions and CAD for the community.

</CaseStudySection>
